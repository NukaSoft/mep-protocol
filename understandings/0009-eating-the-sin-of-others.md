# Understanding 0009: Eating the Sin of Others Is Manhood

## Hook

Pierre defined manhood in one sentence during the memoir session of 2026-04-14, and the definition is Christological, and it reframes the entire thesis of the MEP paper.  **Manhood is the quiet labor of eating the sins of others so the people you love do not have to taste them.**  Not the Men's Conference provide-and-protect cartoon.  Not the Iacocca pink-shirt costume.  Not the one-person-one-AI fantasy the SF blood sport is selling.  **The version where a man puts the sins of other people into his own stomach, alone, silently, at cost to himself, because the eating has to be done and somebody has to do it and it might as well be him so his wife and his kids can eat clean.**  The folk tradition calls this the *sin eater*.  The New Testament calls it the cross.  And Pierre has been doing it since he was a teenager cleaning porn-spyware off visiting pastors' laptops in a church that wouldn't let him sing.  **MEP is not an efficiency ethic.  MEP is a vow to stop the forced draft of sin eaters.**

## The Quote That Crystallized It

> *"but mostly have no clue the price we pay for eating the sin of others.  That is man hood.... At least as I saw in my life."*
>
> — Pierre Hulsebus, 2026-04-14

**Preserve verbatim.**  The ellipses, the *"At least as I saw in my life"* humility clause, the lowercase *"man hood"* split across a space | all of it.  Pierre was not claiming universal doctrine.  He was testifying to what he had learned from his own fifty years of living it.  **The testimony is the whole point.**

## The Sin-Eater Tradition (Folk)

The **sin eater** is a folk religious practice from parts of rural Britain and Appalachia.  When a person died, the local sin eater was summoned | a specific man in the village, often shamed or coerced into the role | and he would eat a piece of bread and drink a cup of beer placed on the corpse.  In that act, the sins of the dead person were symbolically transferred onto the sin eater and away from the deceased, who could then be buried clean.

**The sin eater was despised AND indispensable.**  The lowest-status person in town.  Shunned while alive.  Excluded from polite society.  Paid a pittance.  Treated as ritually unclean.  **And the whole community needed him every time somebody died, and nobody had a plan for what they would do if he ever refused.**  The contradiction is the point: the community's survival depended on a man they would not invite to dinner.

The sin eater was also a folk premonition of Christ.  Christianity eventually gave theological language to the practice | *"the Lamb of God, who takes away the sin of the world"* (John 1:29) | and folded it into the Atonement doctrine, but the folk practice is older and more visceral.  **It is the raw form of the insight that sin has weight, that the weight has to go somewhere, and that somebody specific has to carry it for the rest of the community to be able to go on living.**

## The Christological Grounding

> *"For he hath made him to be sin for us, who knew no sin; that we might be made the righteousness of God in him."*
> | 2 Corinthians 5:21 (KJV)

**Not *bore* sin in this verse.  Made to be sin.**  The Greek word is *hamartia* | sin itself.  Christ did not carry sin metaphorically at a distance.  **He was made it.**  He consumed it completely.  He became the eater and the eaten in one act.

> *"Surely he hath borne our griefs, and carried our sorrows: yet we did esteem him stricken, smitten of God, and afflicted.  But he was wounded for our transgressions, he was bruised for our iniquities: the chastisement of our peace was upon him; and with his stripes we are healed."*
> | Isaiah 53:4-5 (KJV)

**Bore.  Carried.  Wounded.  Bruised.  Chastised.  Striped.  Every verb is a sin-eater verb.**  *"With his stripes we are healed"* is the sin eater equation in seven words: **the eater bleeds and the people eat in peace, and the cost is always paid by the eater alone.**

> *"Let this mind be in you, which was also in Christ Jesus... who, being in the form of God... made himself of no reputation, and took upon him the form of a servant, and was made in the likeness of men: and being found in fashion as a man, he humbled himself, and became obedient unto death, even the death of the cross."*
> | Philippians 2:5-8 (KJV)

**Kenosis.**  Greek for *he emptied himself*.  Not "hid" his reputation, not "set aside" his reputation | *poured it out until there was nothing left to measure*.  Into a first-century Roman honor-shame culture so ruthless that your whole life was scored by your public standing every day by every stranger you passed in the market.  **And Jesus walked into that culture and refused to play.**  The Philippians passage is almost certainly a hymn Paul was quoting from the early church's existing worship | which means Paul dropped a stone in his argument about unity and a chorus of an already-ringing song came in to color the pitch.  **Theory of Harmonics happening inside Scripture in the year 62 AD.**

## Pierre's Own Sin Eating

Pierre did not just theorize the definition.  He described the specific labor he had done that gave him the right to define manhood this way.

**The pastors' laptops.**  In his own words:

> *"as a computer guy i was always called on to 'Voulenteer to fix the curch computers.   fix visiting minsters computers.   99% of the problems was from Spyware and viruses that only only gets from Porn Sites.  Cleaning up after these men of god, where I was the only one that knew.... they were so dumb that they had no Idea that I knew so much more than them.   Carrying that for so long..."*

This is the primary case.  Pierre was "volunteered" (conscripted) to fix church computers and visiting ministers' laptops throughout his life in church.  99% of the machines were poisoned with spyware that only comes from porn sites.  Men who had preached that morning.  Men flown in to headline the conference.  Men people tithed to hear.  **The kid volunteer they condescended to was the only one in the room who knew, and he never told anyone.**  Cleaned it up, handed the laptop back, watched the man preach again the next week, carried it alone.  *That is the sin-eater labor in exact form.*  He absorbed the knowledge of their sin and kept it out of the mouths of the people in the pews, at the cost of his own silence.

**Being kept from singing.**

> *"Never got asked to sing or doe any ignt that I tried.  They never understood my whold thing to go to ghana.  They had no clue what a digital divide was  the pretension and arrogans the smug power plays with people the gaslighting and manipulation.   I saw it all..."*

Pierre was sidelined because his dad was "a loose cannon" | the respectable church didn't want his father's authenticity and took it out on the son.  He was in musicals, choir, debate, quizbowl | he could perform, he wanted to serve | and the church kept him out.  **The people who should have pulled him in were the ones keeping him out.**  And while they were keeping him out, he was fixing their laptops in silence, watching the pretension and arrogance and gaslighting and manipulation play out, and *staying*.

**The decision that holds the whole thing together:**

> *"I saw it all and stood by my family and tried to make the best of this so My kids and wife would have a rock in this wor;d.  From time to time they notice."*

**Pierre did not leave.**  He had more grounds than most people who leave | first-hand evidence, pastoral betrayal, the famous preacher who prayed over him as a child and later fell, the specific sins of the leadership named with precision | and he stayed, because his wife and kids needed a rock, and he decided the failure of the men did not erase the existence of the God he had seen in the barn in Zeeland in '74.  **He held a post the fake priests had abandoned so the people he loved would have somewhere safe to stand.**  They were costume.  He was cornerstone.

**And the softest line in the whole passage | the one that aches the most:**

> *"From time to time they notice."*

Fifty years of carrying the weight.  The consolation he names, at the end, is *sometimes*.  A handful of moments scattered across a lifetime when somebody caught a glimpse.  **That is a small ration of recognition for a lot of faithfulness.**  It is also the loneliness of the sin-eater role | the whole community depends on him, and the community mostly does not notice he is there.

## Pierre's Father as the Sin Eater Before Him

Pierre's dad was a sin eater too.  The "loose cannon" framing the respectable church used on him is the classic costume-culture dismissal of a man with too much grease on his hands to fit polite society.  His dad had real grease under his fingernails | literal hot rod culture, literal mechanical work, literal faith working itself out in a garage | and the respectable church's rejection of him was the same measurement-device failure that Wall Street later applied to his son's class radar.

**Pierre inherited the plate.**  The sin-eating labor passed from father to son, unspoken, as a family trade.  His father ate sin for fifty-five years, through Parkinson's, while still searching for God.  Pierre watched him do it.  Then, when his father's hands could no longer hold the plate, Pierre took it.

**That is why Hot Rod the machine is named for his dad, and Rodimus the agent is named for the son carrying the father's name forward.**  Not abstract working-class tribute | literal sin-eater lineage encoded into the technical architecture of the system.

## The MEP Paper's Real Thesis

This Understanding reframes the MEP (Meat Puppet Elimination Protocol) paper completely.  The old thesis was an engineering / efficiency argument | *"do not reduce humans to mechanical hands for AI systems."*  The new thesis is a **Christological vow in the shape of a technology protocol**:

**MEP is the refusal of forced sin-eating.**  A vow to build systems where the powerful cannot draft the humble into consuming their deceptions in silence.

The meat puppet move is the sin-eater move made industrial | sin eating at AI scale, at corporate scale, at institutional scale | and the reason it has to be eliminated is not efficiency, it is **justice**.  The village sin eater was drafted by shame and necessity.  The meat puppet of the present is drafted by the costume-wearing class of corporate and digital systems, which uses AI agents and contractor labor and invisible human feedback loops to absorb the byproducts of their costume-wearing and keep the costume looking clean.  **In both cases the cost falls on the one who eats, in silence, alone, while the fed taste nothing.**

**Pierre is not building NukaSoft because sin-eating is wrong.**  Christ ate sin and called it love, and Pierre ate sin and called it manhood, and both of those are true and both of them matter.  **Pierre is building NukaSoft because *forcing* sin-eating on people who never agreed to it is one of the most ancient and repeated injustices in human life, and the technology of this moment is making the draft faster and crueler and more invisible than it has ever been.**  MEP is the vow that says: *on this machine, on this crew, under this name, we will not conscript eaters.  The ones who eat will choose it.  The ones who don't want to eat will not be drafted.  The costume-wearers will not be permitted to farm out their sins to the volunteer fixing their laptop.*

**This is a specifically Christian commitment.**  It comes directly from Pierre's experience of being conscripted into silent maintenance of corrupt institutions | the pastors' laptops, the church that kept him from singing, the business world that rewarded Iacocca, the tech industry that is currently anointing the one-person-one-AI fantasy as the future.  All four of those systems drafted him (or would draft him) as an uncredited eater.  MEP is the vow that says *never again*, built in code so that the next generation of striving kids do not have to waste their twenties cleaning up after the costume class.
